# Enterprise Use Cases

*Source: ONUG Agentic AI Overlay Working Group*

## Overview

The working group is collecting practical, domain-specific scenarios that illustrate where an agentic AI overlay adds value. These use cases drive requirements and guide the architecture's evolution. Most use cases are focused on agentic AI **production enablement**.

## Initial Use Cases

1. **Agentic Wealth Advisory** — In wealth management, AI agents coordinate across market data feeds, CRM systems, and compliance engines to deliver personalized, compliant portfolio recommendations in real time.

2. **Automated Incident Detection and Response** — Distributed agents at edge sites detect anomalies in telemetry, apply semantic analysis to classify the event, and coordinate remediation across Network Operations Center/Security Operations Center (NOC/SOC) systems without human delay.

3. **Cross-Cloud Data Sovereignty Enforcement** — AI agents apply policy checks before data leaves a jurisdiction, ensuring compliance with GDPR/PCI while still enabling cross-cloud workflows.

4. **AI-Enhanced Supply Chain Visibility** — Agents embedded in logistics and warehouse systems share context securely to optimize routing, reduce delays, and improve forecasting accuracy.

5. **System of Record Modernization** — Replacing legacy Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) applications with agent-driven AI workflows that can self-adapt to changes in process, data schema, or regulation.

## Top 10 Use Cases (from Community Poll)

During the winter of 2025/26, the working group conducted a survey of the broad ONUG Community and found that poll responses overwhelmingly describe business use cases rooted in operational resilience, regulatory compliance, and controlled automation — rather than customer-facing or transformational AI initiatives.

### 1. Global Regulatory-Compliant AI Operations

Enterprises deploy agentic AI across multiple countries while enforcing country-specific AI regulations, data residency rules, and governance requirements at runtime. Agents adjust behavior based on geography and regulatory context without requiring separate systems per jurisdiction.

### 2. Autonomous Infrastructure Monitoring with Controlled Action

Agents continuously observe infrastructure, applications, and networks to detect anomalies, risks, and failures while distinguishing between passive observation and actions that generate load or side effects. Automated actions are rate-limited, policy-governed, and recoverable to protect availability.

### 3. AI-Driven Incident Response for Mission-Critical Systems

During outages or security incidents, agents coordinate diagnostics, containment, and recovery steps across cloud and on-prem environments. Built-in failover, logging, and human override ensure incidents are resolved quickly without introducing new risk.

### 4. Secure Agent Operations in Highly Regulated Environments

Financial services, healthcare, and other regulated enterprises deploy agentic AI only where full auditability, explainability, and policy enforcement are guaranteed. This enables AI adoption without violating fiduciary duty, client trust, or regulatory obligations.

### 5. Hybrid and Multi-Cloud AI Operations without Vendor Lock-In

Enterprises operate agentic AI consistently across hybrid and multi-cloud environments using loosely coupled, vendor-neutral architectures. This allows organizations to scale AI adoption while maintaining architectural flexibility and negotiating leverage.

### 6. Human-Governed Automation for High-Risk Actions

Agents execute routine operational tasks autonomously but require human approval for privileged, irreversible, or safety-critical actions. This preserves accountability and ensures humans remain in control as AI becomes more embedded in operations.

### 7. Enterprise-Grade AI Observability and Forensics

Organizations gain full visibility into agent behavior through human-readable logs that capture prompts, decisions, tool calls, and outcomes. This enables forensic reconstruction, compliance audits, and continuous improvement of AI systems.

### 8. Resilient AI Operations with Deterministic Failure Modes

Agentic systems are designed to fail safely through redundancy, execution limits, and operational kill switches. This ensures AI-driven workflows degrade gracefully rather than unpredictably in production environments.

### 9. Secure Agent Deployment Across Security Zones

Agents are segmented by enterprise security zones and network domains, preventing unrestricted access across systems or environments. Zero-trust enforcement ensures every interaction is authenticated, authorized, and continuously evaluated.

### 10. Responsible AI Adoption at Enterprise Scale

Enterprises treat agents as long-lived operational assets subject to the same onboarding, testing, approval, and lifecycle management as enterprise applications. This enables controlled, incremental adoption of agentic AI without over-engineering or stalling innovation.
